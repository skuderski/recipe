Pierogi

Pierogi are filled dumplings, made by wrapping unleavened dough around a filling, and occasionally flavored with a savory or sweet garnish and cooking in boiling water.

Ingredients:

  • Dough
  • Minced meat
  • Onions
  • Garlic

Steps:

  1. Dice the onion, finely chop the garlic (with a knife and not a garlic press).
  2. Heat the oil in a large frying pan, and the onions, add a pinch of salt and cook for 5-10 minutes until softened.
  3. Add the meat and 1/4 cup of broth. Cook until warmed up. Season with salt and pepper. Add more broth if the mixture is too dry.
  4. Divide the dough into 2 parts.
  5. Fold the dough over the filling to create a half-moon shape. Press edges together, sealing and crimping with your fingers.
  6. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook the pierogi in batches (for a 8-inch (21 cm) pot I cook about 10-12 dumplings at a time). When they float to the water surface cook them for 1 minute, then remove from the water with a slotted spoon.
